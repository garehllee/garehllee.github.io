The Hyperlink Rabbit Hole

When you read the news, you may encounter a lot of hyperlinks.

Okay

Preliminary research has demonstrated that hyperlinks make readers feel like articles are more credible.

Okay

Primarily, hyperlinks serve three purposes...

1. Linking Facts to Support an Opinion

2. Fair Report Privilege

(Reporting what another individual has already reported)

3. Providing Context

Okay

But how many of us actually click on links? Where do they really link to?

Okay

I chose articles on the same topic (immigration) from the New York Times, Fox News, and MS Now. I tracked each article’s hyperlinks down three generations to understand more about where the news rabbit holes lead. I’ve visualized how hyperlinks can serve to keep us in political media siloes, particularly as the three stories share very few of the same primary sources, despite their topical overlap.
