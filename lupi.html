A celebration of my design hero, Giorgia Lupi’s, work and data humanism philosophy through a poster, booklet, and video.


TIMELINE-

14 weeks

ROLE-

Sole Designer

INTRODUCTION

“What can we learn from this on a more human level—and what does it mean to the world?”

Giorgia Lupi is an information designer and Partner at Pentagram whose work aims to bridge the divide between data and the humanity they represent, taking the abstract and making it felt. Her approach, “data humanism,” recognizes that data isn’t perfect — it’s collected by and for imperfect people. In the flaws of data lies the context and humanity that turns data into a tool that connects, exposes, and resonates.


1. Poster (31.25 x 19.75”)

DATA VISUALIZATION

Visualizing Giorgia Lupi’s Impact

Since Giorgia Lupi is an information designer, no project celebrating her would be complete without a data visualization. I wanted to investigate her impact, both numerically and qualitatively, landing on colorful squares to depict individual pieces. Behind the data lies the tenants of her data humanism manifesto. 2. Booklet (8.25x10.5” Trim Size)

STORYTELLING

Telling Lupi’s Story

While the poster is a surface-level introduction to Lupi, the booklet is an opportunity to dive deep into her life, work, philosophy, and legacy (thusfar). I brought through the progression motif of circles and polygons. I used organic shapes to symbolize the messiness of real-life as it permeates data and corporate structures, symbolized by rigid polygons. I selected PP Editorial and PP Nikkei Maru for an edgier take on Lupi’s usual airy style. Read the full process writeup on Medium,here.
